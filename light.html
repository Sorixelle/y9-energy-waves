Light Waves

How is infrared radiation used in remote controls?

When you push a button on your remote control the infrared LED on the front of your remote starts flashing on and off rapidly. Because humans can't see infrared light, we don't see it happen. The appliance then detects that light with an infrared reciever, and performs the action the button is mapped to. But how does the appliance know what button was pressed, or if the signal was directed at it? The infrared light flashes have this information. There are many different protocols of infrared light transmission, but the SIRC (Sony Infrared Light Code) is one of the most widely used. The signal begins with a longer "start" pulse, which signifies that the signal is the beginning of a command. After that, the light pulses on and off, representng binary digits. The amount of time the light is on for determines if the pulse is a 1 or 0; a pulse representing 1 is 2 times longer than one representing 0. After the command, an address is sent, which tells the appliance what device the signal was meant for.

How does radiation therapy treat cancer?

Radiation therapy uses high-energy waves such as x-rays, gamma rays and charged particles to kill cancer cells. The waves kill cancer cells by damaging the DNA in the cells or creating charged particles in the cell, which in turn damages the cell's DNA. If the cell's DNA is damaged beyond repair, the cell becomes unable to divide into new cells, and dies. The cells are then broken down and eliminated by the body's natural processes. Radiation therapy can kill normal cells along with cancer cells, so side effects may occur. This is why doctors take time to plan a radiation therapy treatment for a patient.

Why does a swimmer's leg look distorted underwater?

A swimmer's leg is distorted underwater because of refraction. Refraction occurs when light is travelling from one medium from another; in this example, light is travelling from air to water. Because water is denser than air, light slows down in the water. This causes the light to change direction. Because the light is travelling in a divverent direction, it reaches the eye in a different direction as well, causing the swimmer's leg to appear distorted and not in the place it actually is.

How was the bionic eye developed?

The bionic eye is a device that allows blind people to see. It works by using a camera attached to a pair of glasses that transmits radio signals at a high frequency to a microchip implanted in the eye. The microchip converts the signals into electrical impulses that stimulate the retina and pass the signals to the brain. The bionic eye benefits people with retinis pigmentosa (loss of photoreceptor cells and peripheral vision) and macular degeneration, which causes cells in the retina to stop converting light into signals, leaving the afflicted person unable to form a complete image.
