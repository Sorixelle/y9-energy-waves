How are television signals transmitted?

Television signals are transmitted on 2 waves per channel: an AM wave for video, and a FM wave for audio. The television uses the antenna to recieve those radio waves, and then takes the two waves and transforms them into visual and audio signals, and plays them through the screen and speakers.
