Glossary

  • Medium: The material a wave travels through.
  • Energy Wave: Particles moving in a wave to transmit energy.
  • Transverse Wave: A wave that moves up and down, or left and right.
  • Compression Wave: A wave that moves back and forth, compressing itself.
  • Frequency: The amount of times a wave repeats a pattern in a given amount of time.
  • Wavelength: The distance between a wave repetition.
  • Amplitude: How high the wave gets.
  • Electromagnetic Spectrum: The range of all types of electromagnetic waves.
  • Visible Light: Light that is in a range that the human eye can see.
  • Infrared Radiation: Light before red on the spectrum. Commonly used in close-range communication devices.
  • Ultra-violet Radiation: Light after violet on the spectrum. Emitted by the Sun.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Waves that can travel through a vacuum.
  • Reflection: Light bouncing off an object.
  • Refraction: Light travelling through one medium to another, causing it to bend.
  • Concave: An inward-facing curve.
  • Convex: An outward-facing curve.
  • Compressions: A compression wave moving closer together.
  • Rarefactions: A compression wave moving further apart.
  • Sound Waves: A wave that can be heard by an animal.
  • Reverberation: Large amounts of sound waves reflecting to create an echoing effect.
  • Radio Waves: A wave with a wavelength longer than infrared light.
  • Transmitter: A device that creates and sends out radio waves.
  • Amplitude Modulation: Changing the amplitude of a carrier wave.
  • Frequency Modulation: Changing the frequency of a carrier wave.
  • Wireless Communication: Sending messages using a method that does not require any cables.
  • Microwaves: Radio waves with short wavelengths.
  • Coaxial Cables: A type of cable that can transmit multiple signals at once.
  • Optical Fibre: A typr of cable that uses light to transmit information.
  • Satellite: A machine orbiting Earth that recieves signals and sends them back to another place on Earth.
